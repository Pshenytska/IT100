Hum Bao

Student Emily

Hum Bao is a traditional dim sum dish from China. Dated back all the way from around 475- 221 B.C.E. There are many varieties of Hum Bao that can be found in Korea, Malaysia, Japan, and the Filipines as well. Bao is made from flour, water, sugar, milk, oil and yeast. The usual bread ingredients, where the difference is is how it is cooked. Instead of baking it in an oven it is steamed. For this reason they come out plump, fluffy and cloud-like fist sized dumplings. Hum bao is steamed with some sort of filling inside of it.
